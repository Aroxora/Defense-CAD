# Heavy Cruiser / Large Surface Combatant Proposal

**Classification: UNCLASSIFIED // CONCEPTUAL DESIGN**

## Executive Summary

Current US Navy destroyers (Arleigh Burke-class, ~9,800 tons) and cruisers (Ticonderoga-class, ~9,600 tons) have limited deck space and tonnage capacity for integrating larger Army systems. This proposal outlines specifications for a **Large Surface Combatant (LSC)** in the 20,000-25,000 ton displacement range that could:

1. Carry significantly larger radar arrays and power generation
2. Accommodate land-based air defense systems (THAAD-class interceptors)
3. Provide sufficient deck space for larger directed energy weapons
4. Host Army long-range precision fires (LRPF) systems

---

## Problem Statement

Army systems designed for land deployment often exceed naval integration constraints:

| System | Weight | Footprint | Power Requirement | Naval Fit Issue |
|--------|--------|-----------|-------------------|-----------------|
| THAAD Launcher | 40 tons | 12m x 3m | 200 kW | Too heavy for DDG deck |
| AN/TPY-2 Radar | 25 tons | 9m x 9m trailer | 600 kW | Exceeds DDG power margin |
| LTAMDS Radar | 15 tons | 6m x 6m | 400 kW | Deck space limited |
| PrSM Launcher | 20 tons | 10m x 3m | 50 kW | VLS incompatible |

## Proposed Specifications: LSC-X Heavy Cruiser

### Hull Characteristics

| Parameter | Value | Rationale |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Displacement (full load) | 22,000-25,000 tons | 2.5x DDG for payload capacity |
| Length Overall | 210 m (689 ft) | Accommodate large deck systems |
| Beam | 28 m (92 ft) | Stability for large radar arrays |
| Draft | 9.5 m (31 ft) | Compatible with major ports |
| Speed (sustained) | 28+ knots | Carrier battle group integration |
| Range | 8,000 nm at 18 knots | Extended Pacific operations |

### Power Generation

| System | Capacity | Notes |
|--------|----------|-------|
| Gas Turbines | 4x LM2500+G4 (100 MW total) | Proven marinized engines |
| Integrated Power System | 80 MW available | Full electric drive |
| Auxiliary Generators | 4x 4 MW diesel | Redundancy |
| Power for Weapons/Sensors | 25-30 MW reserved | DEW and radar headroom |

**Comparison**: DDG-51 Flight III provides ~78 MW total, ~9 MW for combat systems.

### Weapons Integration

#### Vertical Launch System

| VLS Type | Cells | Compatible Munitions |
|----------|-------|---------------------|
| Mk 41 Strike-Length | 128 | SM-2, SM-6, TLAM, ESSM |
| Mk 57 (enlarged) | 32 | SM-3 Block IIA, future hypersonics |
| **Total VLS** | **160** | vs. 96 on DDG-51 Flight III |

#### Deck-Mounted Systems (New Capability)

| System | Quantity | Notes |
|--------|----------|-------|
| THAAD-ER Launcher | 2 | 16 interceptors total |
| Containerized PrSM | 4 | 16 missiles, rapid reload |
| 150 kW-class Laser | 2 | CIWS augmentation |
| 30mm CIWS | 4 | Phalanx replacement |

### Sensor Suite

| Radar | Type | Capability |
|-------|------|------------|
| AN/SPY-7(V)1 | S-band AESA | Primary air/missile defense |
| AN/TPY-2 (marinized) | X-band | BMD discrimination |
| AN/SPQ-9B | X-band | Horizon search |
| SLQ-32(V)7 | EW suite | Full spectrum awareness |

**Key Advantage**: Co-located S-band and X-band provides discrimination capability currently requiring separate Aegis and THAAD assets.

## Operational Concept

### Role 1: Distributed Maritime BMD

Deploy LSC-X forward to provide THAAD-equivalent protection for allied territories without land basing political constraints.

```
                    THREAT AXIS
                        |
                        v
    [DPRK/PRC SRBM/MRBM Launch]
                        |
                        |  1200 km
                        |
                        v
              +------------------+
              |    LSC-X #1      |
              | AN/TPY-2 + SPY-7 |
              |  THAAD-ER x16    |
              +------------------+
                     200 nm
              +------------------+
              |    LSC-X #2      |
              |   SM-3 IIA x32   |
              +------------------+
                        |
                        |
                     DEFENDED AREA
                   (Japan/Guam/etc)
```

### Role 2: Army-Navy Fires Integration

Host Army LRPF systems for maritime strike without requiring land access:

| Mission | Army System | Naval Benefit |
|---------|-------------|---------------|
| Anti-ship | PrSM Increment 4 | 1000+ km range |
| Land attack | ATACMS/PrSM | Precision fires from sea |
| Airfield denial | GMLRS-ER | Runway cratering |

### Role 3: High-Value Unit Escort

Provide dedicated BMD umbrella for carrier strike groups, replacing the need for multiple DDGs in BMD picket roles.

## Risk Assessment

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|------|------------|------------|
| Cost overrun | Medium | Evolutionary design, proven systems |
| Schedule slip | Medium | No new hull form (scaled DDG-1000) |
| Technical integration | Low | All systems TRL 7+ |
| Survivability concerns | Medium | Size enables better compartmentalization |

---

## Recommendation

Proceed with conceptual design study for LSC-X class, leveraging:

1. **DDG-1000 hull form** (scaled up 15%) - existing tooling, reduced risk
2. **DDG-51 Flight III combat system** - proven BMD capability
3. **Army PEO Missiles collaboration** - THAAD/PrSM integration requirements
4. **IPS from DDG-1000** - proven integrated power system

This approach provides the "100x battleship power" capability through sensor/effector integration rather than simply scaling gun systems - a more relevant measure of naval combat power in the missile age.
