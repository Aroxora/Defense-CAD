Simulation Methodology

Random Variables: Detection range (log-normal), track quality (beta), missile guidance error (Rayleigh), fuze function (Bernoulli), warhead lethality (empirical CDF).

Propagation Model: ITU-R P.528 for air-to-air paths with standard atmosphere. Rain attenuation per ITU-R P.838 at 4 mm/hr (moderate rain scenario).

Countermeasures: F-35 ECM modeled as 3-6 dB J/S (jammer-to-signal ratio) reducing Pk by 15-30%. Chaff/flare effectiveness: 20-40% against radar/IR seekers.

Key Finding: Multi-ship passive triangulation (2× J-20) provides highest Pk at 43%, while F-35 operating in EMCON reduces Pk to 8%. The sidelobe exploitation scenario yields Pk = 24.5%, viable when F-35 is actively scanning.

Strategic Assessment

While the F-35 has multiple countermeasures available, the fundamental physics of radar sidelobe emissions creates an inherent vulnerability. The most effective counter is emissions control (EMCON), but this limits the F-35's situational awareness and requires reliance on offboard sensors.

Recommendation: Develop network-centric tactics where F-35s operate with minimal emissions while receiving targeting data from platforms like E-2D, F/A-18G, or ground-based sensors. This preserves stealth while maintaining combat effectiveness.
