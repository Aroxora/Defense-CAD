On December 22, 2025, President Trump announced the "Golden Fleet" program at Mar-a-Lago: a new class of guided missile battleships (BBG) displacing 30,000–40,000 tons, the largest U.S. surface combatants since WWII. The lead ship, USS Defiant (BBG-1), would carry 128 Mk 41 VLS cells, 12 CPS hypersonic missiles, electromagnetic railguns, directed energy weapons, and nuclear-armed cruise missiles. This section provides an honest engineering assessment of the proposal's feasibility and value to taxpayers.

Bottom Line for Taxpayers

  • Total program cost: $180–225 billion for 20–25 ships at ~$9B each. This exceeds the Columbia-class SSBN program ($128B) — the Navy's current top priority.
  • The railgun was already canceled. The Navy spent $500M+ and couldn't solve barrel erosion. Restarting adds billions with no guarantee of success.
  • SLCM-N has no warhead and no funding. Congress defunded the nuclear cruise missile. Resuming requires NNSA warhead development — a decade-long effort.
  • Existing platforms already deliver CPS. DDG-1000 (3 hulls refitting now) and Virginia Block V SSNs (building) carry CPS without a new $9B ship class.
  • Contradicts the Navy's own strategy. Distributed maritime operations calls for many networked platforms, not a few massive targets. China's DF-21D/DF-26 ASBMs are purpose-built to sink exactly this kind of ship.
  • Shipyard capacity does not exist. U.S. naval shipyards are already 2-3 years behind on submarines, carriers, and destroyers. A 35,000-ton new design requires facilities and workforce that haven't been built.
  • CSIS verdict: "This ship will never sail." Historical pattern — DDG-1000 cut from 24→3, CG(X) canceled, LCS truncated — predicts cancellation after billions spent on design.
  • USS Zumwalt already carries 12 CPS and completed sea trials Jan 2026. All 3 Zumwalts + Virginia SSNs at Pearl Harbor by mid-2028. The CPS mission is covered. The BBG adds zero hypersonic capacity over what already exists.

Historical Context: Why the 1990s Arsenal Ship Failed

The Navy-DARPA Arsenal Ship program (1996-1997) proposed a 500-VLS, 50-crew, $450M ship. It was canceled because:

  • Political: Congress couldn't determine which service would own it. Navy lost enthusiasm after Admiral Boorda's death.
  • Tactical: Putting 500 missiles on one hull = one target. Loss of one ship = loss of 500 cells.
  • Alternative existed: Ohio SSGN conversion gave 154 Tomahawks per boat with submarine survivability for ~$1B/hull.

Lesson: Distributed is better than concentrated. The Ohio SSGN model — converting existing hulls — worked. The Trump-class BBG repeats the Arsenal Ship's mistakes without learning from its failure.

Power Analysis: Which Platforms Can Support 100+ MW DEW?

Directed energy weapons at "battleship-relevant" power levels (MW-class lasers, railguns) require massive electrical generation. Most surface combatants cannot provide this. Only integrated electric propulsion (IPS) ships or nuclear vessels have the margin.

Bottom Line: Only DDG-1000 Zumwalt has the power margin for high-power DEW among existing surface combatants. If the Navy needs 100+ MW DEW platforms, the options are: (1) use the 3 Zumwalts, (2) build new IPS ships, or (3) use nuclear vessels. The Trump-class BBG proposes conventional propulsion — it cannot meet the DEW power requirement without a complete propulsion redesign.

Recommended Architecture: DDG-1000 + MASC Hybrid

Tier 1: CPS Strike + DEW (DDG-1000 Zumwalt)

  • 3 hulls × 12 CPS = 36 CPS total
  • 78 MW IPS → 300-600 kW laser DEW capable
  • Already built, refitting now, sea trials complete Jan 2026
  • Role: Hypersonic strike, DEW defense, C2 for MASC

Tier 2: Distributed Magazine (MASC Fleet)

  • 6-12 unmanned vessels × 16-32 Mk 41 VLS = 96-384 cells
  • SM-6, Tomahawk, LRASM for conventional strike
  • ~$200-350M per hull, commercial standards, 18-month delivery
  • Role: Adjunct magazine, distributed lethality, attritable

Tier 3: Subsurface Strike (Virginia Block V)

  • Each boat: 4 CPS (VPM) + conventional torpedoes/Tomahawk
  • Highest survivability, nuclear endurance
  • Continue production as fast as industrial base allows
  • Role: Survivable CPS reserve, high-threat environments

Total Capacity: 36 CPS (DDG-1000) + 96-384 Mk 41 (MASC) + 4+ CPS per Virginia = 136-420+ strike cells distributed across 10-20+ platforms. No single point of failure. DDG-1000 provides DEW power. MASC provides distributed magazine. Virginia provides survivable reserve. All using existing/funded programs. Zero need for a $9B battleship.

Cost Analysis

Honest cost comparison. Ohio SSGN retention has the lowest marginal cost but faces hull life limits. Virginia Block V is the highest cost per CPS cell but offers submarine survivability. DDG-1000 is a sunk cost with moderate CPS capacity. LSC-X cost is speculative — new-build programs routinely exceed estimates by 50-100%.

Best Value (if feasible)

Ohio SSGN Retention

44+ CPS cells across 4 hulls. Zero acquisition cost — hulls exist. But reactor refueling and hull life extension required. Navy has chosen to retire these hulls.

Lowest Risk

DDG-1000 Refit + Virginia Block V

Both programs are funded and underway. DDG-1000 provides 36 CPS cells (3 hulls x 12). Virginia VPM adds 4 cells per boat. Mixed surface/subsurface force provides operational flexibility.

Highest Risk

LSC-X New Build

No program of record. Estimated $5-8B per hull is speculative. Historical precedent (DDG-1000, LCS, FFG-62) shows new combatant programs face significant cost growth and schedule delays.

Engineering Notes: What's Proven vs. Speculative

  • Proven: C-HGB flight test success (Dec 2024). Cold-gas launch from canister. DDG-1000 CPS integration design.
  • Proven: Virginia VPM large-diameter tube design (derived from Ohio SSGN tubes).
  • In Development: DDG-1000 weapons module refit. At-sea CPS launch (not yet tested from ship).
  • Speculative: LSC-X hull design. 12-20 CPS cell surface launcher. Cost estimates for new-build program.
  • Speculative: CPS effectiveness against defended targets (no combat use). Actual P(k) against hardened/mobile targets.
