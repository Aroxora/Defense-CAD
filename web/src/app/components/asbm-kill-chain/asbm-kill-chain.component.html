🛰 Trajectory Profiles vs BMD Envelopes

Key Insight: The DF-17 HGV flies a depressed boost-glide trajectory at 40-60 km altitude, below the SM-3 engagement envelope (>100 km) and above SM-6 effective ceiling (~50 km). This creates a "seam" in layered BMD that traditional ballistic trajectories (DF-21D, DF-26) cannot exploit. The HGV maintains Mach 10+ speed with 5-10g lateral maneuverability throughout the glide phase.

Glide Range Equation

R = V² × (L/D) / g

Glide range as a function of velocity, lift-to-drag ratio, and gravitational acceleration. Higher L/D ratios extend range but reduce maneuverability.

Variables:
  • R = Glide range (m)
  • V = Vehicle velocity (m/s)
  • L/D = Lift-to-drag ratio (dimensionless)
  • g = Gravitational acceleration (9.81 m/s²)

Stagnation Point Heat Flux

q = ρ × V³ / (2 × Rn)

Aerodynamic heating at the stagnation point. Heat flux scales with the cube of velocity, making thermal protection the primary engineering challenge for HGVs.

Variables:
  • q = Heat flux (W/m²)
  • ρ = Atmospheric density (kg/m³)
  • V = Vehicle velocity (m/s)
  • Rn = Nose radius (m)

Position Uncertainty

σ = Vship × tstale

Target position uncertainty grows linearly with data staleness. A carrier traveling at 30 knots moves ~900 m per minute, requiring frequent sensor updates to maintain targeting accuracy.

Variables:
  • σ = Position uncertainty (m)
  • Vship = Target ship velocity (m/s)
  • tstale = Time since last sensor update (s)
}

Strategic Assessment

The PLA ASBM kill chain depends on a complex sensor-to-shooter network with multiple single points of failure. U.S. countermeasures should target the weakest links: over-the-horizon radar cueing, mid-course guidance updates, and terminal seeker acquisition. A layered defense combining electronic warfare, kinetic intercept (SM-3/SM-6), and deception operations can significantly degrade ASBM effectiveness.

Recommendation: Invest in electronic attack capabilities to disrupt the kill chain at the sensor fusion and mid-course update phases, where the ASBM is most vulnerable to denial and deception.
